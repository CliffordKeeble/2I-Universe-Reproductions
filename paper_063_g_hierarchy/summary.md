# Paper 63 — *G and the Hierarchy Dilemma*

**Status: RETIRED** (June 2026)

## Retired

The central thesis (G a local statistical quantity, varying between
laboratories) was self-falsified three independent ways:

- **Terrestrial envelope** — predicted δG/G ~10⁻¹³ vs observed ~10⁻⁴, ~9 orders short;
- **Mach estimate** — G ≈ Rc²/(N m_p) ≈ 7×10⁻¹¹, showing G is a global constant;
- **Galactic-orbit bound** — local model excluded by LLR/BBN at ~3 orders.

**G is global, not local.** Surviving content (the Mach order-of-magnitude
estimate) and the open question are recorded in `findings.md`. No successor
paper — the open question is not yet a result.

Independent verification (this folder): all three estimates reproduced on
independently sourced inputs (`verify_G.py`, `findings.md`). The numbers
corroborate the brief within order-of-magnitude tolerance.

## OPEN

Does the geometry of S³/2I derive the baryon count N (~10⁸⁰) and the scale/age of
the universe R? If both are geometric, ⟨G⟩ is pinned and `G ≈ Rc²/(N m_p)` becomes
a 2I retrodiction rather than the Sciama–Mach relation with imported cosmic
inputs. Until then, the estimate imports the universe to estimate G. This is the
same hinge as the ⟨G⟩ question in the Fizz Higgs/gravity report — one thread,
three appearances.
